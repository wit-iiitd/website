Open Source

Google Summer of Code

Google Summer of Code is a global program focused on introducing students to open source software development. Students work on a three month long programming project with an open source organization during their break from university.

Eligibility: 18+ University students

Applications Period: March

Stipend: Check here

Outreachy

Outreachy provides internships to work in open source and free software. Outreachy internships are open to applicants around the world. Interns work remotely, and are not required to move.

Eligibility: Women Cis-Trans, residents and nationals of the United States of any gender who are Black/African American, Hispanic/Latin, Native American/American Indian, Alaska Native, Native Hawaiian, or Pacific Islander.

Applications Period: February to March and September to October

Stipend: 5,000 USD

Google Season of Docs

Brings open source and technical writer communities together, to benefit them both. They aim to raise awareness of open source, of docs, and of technical writing.

Eligibility: 18+

GirlsScript Summer of Code

Aims to help beginners get started with Open Source Development while encouraging diversity. Throughout the program, participants contribute to different projects under guidance of experienced mentors.

Eligibility: Everyone

Applications Period: January to February

Stipend: Top participants get exciting goodies and opportunities.

The X.Org Endless Vacation of Code (EVoC)

X.Org Endless Vacation of Code (EVoC) is a Google Summer of Code (GSoC)-like project initiated in 2008 funded by the X.Org Foundation. It allows students to participate to X. Org-related projects during their vacation, at any time of the year.

Eligibility: Anyone can apply. Stipend is only provided to students with age 18+

Applications Period: Anytime throughout the year

Stipend: 5,000 USD
